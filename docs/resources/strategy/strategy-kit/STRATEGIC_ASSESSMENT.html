I
The Thesis
What the CEO proposed and why it might work

The CEO's thesis is straightforward:

The Core Idea

WooCommerce merchants who outgrow the platform have no good options that let them keep WordPress. Commerce.com, with its headless architecture, can be that option.

The Observation

WooCommerce powers approximately 4.5 million online stores globally, representing about 20% of the e-commerce market. StoreLeads 2024 However, WooCommerce has significant churn—merchants who outgrow the platform's capabilities, struggle with performance, or need enterprise features eventually replatform.

When they do, 48% migrate to Shopify. Cart2Cart 2024 This happens almost by default—Shopify has the brand recognition, the marketing, and the migration tools.

The Gap in the Market

What these merchants give up when moving to Shopify:

  • WordPress investment: Years of content, SEO equity, and site customization
  • Flexibility: Shopify's closed ecosystem vs. WordPress's open architecture
  • Control: Platform dependency and transaction fees (0.5-2%)

Many merchants would prefer an option that lets them keep WordPress for content while upgrading their commerce capabilities. That option doesn't exist today in a well-packaged form.

The Commerce.com Angle

Commerce.com (BigCommerce + Feedonomics + Makeswift) is uniquely positioned to fill this gap:

Asset Role in Strategy
BigCommerce Headless commerce engine—works alongside WordPress, not instead of it
Feedonomics Product data syndication—connects to marketplaces without Shopify dependency
Makeswift Visual page builder—enables rapid storefront development on headless
The Value Proposition

"Keep WordPress. Upgrade Commerce."

Why Now?

  • WPEngine tension: Recent conflicts with Automattic have WordPress hosting providers looking for alternatives to WooCommerce dependency
  • Headless maturity: The technology for WordPress + headless commerce is now proven and accessible
  • Market timing: Before competitors recognize and fill this gap

Not all WooCommerce merchants are candidates. The addressable segment is:

III
The Strategy
How Commerce.com would execute

If the thesis holds, how would Commerce.com execute?

Strategic Approach: Stealth Partnership

Rather than a public "WooCommerce migration" campaign (which would invite competitive response), the strategy is a stealth partnership with WordPress hosting providers:

  1. Partner with WPEngine (and potentially Kinsta, Pantheon) as a recommended migration path
  2. Position as "WordPress-native commerce upgrade" rather than "WooCommerce replacement"
  3. Build migration tooling that makes the transition seamless
  4. Enable SI partners to execute migrations at scale

Why Product, Not Services

This initiative should be a product investment, not a services business:

Services Approach (Not Recommended)

  • Run migrations as a consulting business
  • Revenue = project fees
  • Scale by hiring people
  • Compete with SI partners

Product Approach (Recommended)

  • Build migration tooling and platform
  • Value = reduced CAC, platform stickiness
  • Scale by building software
  • Enable SI partners

IV
The Risks
Honest assessment of what could go wrong

We conducted adversarial analysis using 9 skeptical personas and an AI Council review. Here's what could go wrong:

High-Risk Factors

WPEngine Partnership Fails

HIGH

Risk: WPEngine declines to partner, or the partnership doesn't produce referrals.

Mitigation: Phase 0 includes partnership validation by week 4. If WPEngine declines, pivot to alternative channels or abort.

Automattic Response

HIGH

Risk: Automattic improves WooCommerce or makes migration to alternatives harder.

Mitigation: Current Automattic/WPEngine tension creates a window. Move fast. Stealth positioning reduces visibility.

"Keep WordPress" Value Unproven

HIGH

Risk: Merchants don't actually value keeping WordPress enough to choose Commerce.com over Shopify.

Mitigation: Phase 0 pilot migrations will test this directly with real customers.

Medium-Risk Factors

Technical Complexity

MEDIUM

Risk: Migration tooling is harder to build than estimated, or edge cases explode scope.

Mitigation: Phase 0 scopes 3 pilot migrations to surface complexity early. GO/NO-GO gate before Phase 1.

VI
The Recommendation
Go/No-Go framework and next steps

Recommendation: Proceed to Phase 0

Invest $190K over 12 weeks to validate the thesis with real partnerships, real technology, and real customers. Make the scale decision with data, not assumptions.

Why This Recommendation

  1. The opportunity is real: 4.5M WooCommerce stores, 48% defaulting to Shopify, no "Keep WordPress" alternative
  2. The timing is right: WPEngine/Automattic tension creates a window; headless technology is mature
  3. The risk is bounded: $190K validation investment, not scale commitment
  4. The downside is learning: Even if NO-GO, we learn about the market and build migration capabilities
