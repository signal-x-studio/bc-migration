Why "The Offer" Matters Now

The industry is in what Rick Watson calls the "show up and throw up" phase of AI. Lots of companies have AI strategies. Few can explain the business outcome their AI strategy delivers.

BigCommerce's positioning isn't "we have AI." It's: "We help merchants sell more, across more channels, with less effort." AI is how we deliver that - not the headline.

Market Opportunity

Commerce is undergoing its third platform shift:

Era Paradigm BigCommerce Position
2000s Monolithic platforms Challenger to Magento
2010s SaaS + APIs Peer to Shopify
2020s Headless/Composable Invested (Catalyst, Makeswift)
2030s AI-Native Opportunity to lead

Our Right to Win

Open + composable architecture means merchants can use our AI capabilities OR bring their own. Unlike Shopify's walled garden, we enable choice. Our Feedonomics acquisition gives us unique multi-channel distribution capabilities no competitor can match.

BigCommerce has the components (Core + Makeswift + Feedonomics) but lacks unified data, events, and identity layers to deliver AI-native experiences.

What This Enables for Merchants

The "PIM business is the sexiest unsexy business on the planet right now" - Rick Watson, NRF 2026. Product data quality directly impacts marketplace performance, search rankings, and conversion rates.

Why this blocks AI-Native:

  1. No unified data layer - AI needs holistic context (products + content + performance + channels)
  2. No event stream - AI needs real-time signals, not batch sync
  3. No semantic layer - AI needs meaning, not just data
  4. No unified identity - Personalization requires knowing the merchant/shopper across touchpoints
